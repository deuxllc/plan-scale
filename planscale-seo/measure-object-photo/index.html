How to measure an object in a photo

Upload a photo

Use an image of furniture, packaging, a product, a part, or another object.

2

Use a ruler, paper sheet, card, or another object with a known real-world size.

3

Measure dimensions

After calibration, draw measurement lines to estimate other distances in the image.

Accuracy matters

Photos can distort size because of perspective. Best results come from flat objects, straight-on photos, and a reference length in the same plane as the measured object.

Best case

A straight photo, flat object, and visible ruler or known reference object.

Higher error risk

The photo is angled, the object is deep, or the reference is closer to the camera than the measured part.

FAQ

Can I measure an object without a reference?

Not reliably. You need at least one known size in the photo to calibrate the scale.

Can I measure furniture from a photo?

Yes, if the photo includes a known reference and the perspective is not too strong.
